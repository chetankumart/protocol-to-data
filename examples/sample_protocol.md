# Clinical Study Protocol (Synthetic Example)

**Protocol Title:** A Phase 3, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study of
Drug-X in Adults with Chronic Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction (HFrEF)

**Protocol Number:** CARDIO-HF-P3
**Phase:** 3
**Therapeutic Area:** Cardiovascular
**Indication:** Chronic heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF)

## 1. Objectives and Endpoints

**Primary endpoint:** Change from baseline to Week 24 in KCCQ-12 Total Symptom Score.

**Secondary endpoints:**
- Change from baseline in systolic and diastolic blood pressure at each visit.
- Change from baseline in NT-proBNP at Week 12 and Week 24.
- Proportion of subjects with improvement in NYHA functional class at Week 24.

**Safety:** Incidence and severity of treatment-emergent adverse events.

## 2. Study Design

Randomized 1:1 to one of two arms:
- **Drug-X Arm:** Drug-X 40 mg once daily, oral.
- **Placebo Arm:** Matching placebo once daily, oral.

Approximately 40 subjects will be enrolled (this synthetic example is small for demos;
real studies enroll hundreds).

## 3. Visit Schedule

| Visit | Timing |
|-------|--------|
| Screening | Day -14 (± 7 days) |
| Baseline / Randomization / First Dose | Day 1 |
| Week 4 | Day 28 (± 3 days) |
| Week 12 | Day 84 (± 5 days) |
| Week 24 (End of Treatment) | Day 168 (± 7 days) |
| Follow-up | Day 196 (± 7 days) |

## 4. Population

- Adults aged 18–85 years.
- Male and female subjects.
- **Key inclusion:** Documented HFrEF (LVEF ≤ 40%); NYHA class II–III; on stable
  guideline-directed medical therapy for ≥ 4 weeks.
- **Key exclusion:** eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m²; systolic BP < 90 mmHg at screening;
  acute decompensated heart failure within 4 weeks.

## 5. Assessments

- **Vital signs (VS):** blood pressure, pulse at every visit.
- **Laboratory (LB):** NT-proBNP, chemistry, hematology at screening, Week 12, Week 24.
- **Patient-reported outcomes (QS):** KCCQ-12 and NYHA class at baseline, Week 12, Week 24.
- **Exposure (EX):** study drug dosing recorded from Day 1.
- **Adverse events (AE):** collected continuously from first dose onward.
